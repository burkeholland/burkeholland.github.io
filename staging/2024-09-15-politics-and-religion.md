I've avoided touching any political or religious topics thus far in my career. After all, it's best to avoid these topics in polite company. Also, nobody wants to hear yet another opinion these kinds of things - nobody cares. But writing is processing and I've spent a lot of time trying to figure out how religion has betrayed itself for politics, why that happened and what are good religious people to do about it.

In the interest of full disclosure, I am a spiritual man. Faith is important to me and the Bible informs much if not all of my worldview. I was raised in the evangelical church and I remain there today. I think a lot of people will read the word "evangelical" and recoil. And, sadly, I think that reaction is well deserved.

It's my opinion that the evangelical church as defined by America today has lost any moral high ground on any issue. This has happened slowly over the course of many decades, but is punctuated by the almost total support enjoyed by Donald Trump among those who would call themselves Christians.

If you find yourself among the group I just described, you probably won't like this post very much and you may think me someone who has been coopted by "The Left" or "Liberals" or all of my corporate DEI training. Or perhaps I have TDS. Or maybe I just want attention. I am writing a blog post afterall - why not just keep my mouth shut?

I would simply say that the Bible isn't very much about "keeping your mouth shut".

The New Testament Bible is a story of the cosmic redemption of human beings through the death and ressurection of Jesus. Marbled in are instructions and commandments on how to lead a selfless life of service to God. And how does one serve God? By serving others. That is, in a nutshell, the entire message of the gospel. You are redeemed and that redemption frees you to be the hands and feet of a God who loves. Or put in the words of the Bible itself...

> Religion that is pure and undefiled before God the Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their affliction, and to keep oneself unstained from the world.
> James 1:27 ESV

Jesus himself was also quite a-political.
